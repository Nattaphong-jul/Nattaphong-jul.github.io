FERAL Project

This is a horror game project built around the idea of running to survive. Each escape route is procedurally generated, ensuring a different pathway every time you play.

How the idea started

This project began when my friends and I decided to create a game together, and we chose the horror genre. Our inspiration came from one of our favourite games, "Backrooms: Escape Together". In that game, there is a stage where players must run and find a way out while being chased by a creature. We loved the tension and pressure of that moment, so we decided to create our own take on the concept—with our own twist.

Game Story Background

You are a scientist working on a secret project involving a dangerous creature in a hidden laboratory. One day, you hear strange noises coming from the main chamber. When you go to investigate, you discover that the creature the lab had been experimenting on has escaped and is nowhere to be found. The entire facility is silent and empty— except for you.

You decide to escape… but suddenly, the door behind you—one that was locked—slams violently, as if something massive is trying to break through. You then notice something horrifying: structures resembling tree roots, but made of flesh and blood, are spreading across the facility—crawling along the floors and climbing across the ceiling. You sprint through the corridors, desperately searching for an exit before the door gives way and the creature catches up to you.

Game Mechanics

The core gameplay mechanic of FERAL is running to escape while navigating through procedurally generated pathways. Each time you play, the layout of the corridors changes, creating a unique experience with every run.

Throughout the pathways, there are items and structures that the player can use to slow down the creature. The creature cannot be killed or permanently avoided, so the player must keep running and making quick decisions to survive until they reach the Safe Room.
